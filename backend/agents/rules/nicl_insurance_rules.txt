# National Insurance Company Limited - National Mediclaim Plus Policy

## Document Information
- Insurer: National Insurance Company Limited
- CIN: U10200WB1906GOI001713
- IRDAI Registration Number: 58
- Product: National Mediclaim Plus Policy
- Scope: Underwriting Guide (India)

## Product Introduction
National Mediclaim Plus Policy is an individual health insurance policy covering inpatient treatment (cashless/reimbursement) for illness/disease or injury contracted during the policy period.

Core cover includes:
- 30 days pre-hospitalization and 60 days post-hospitalization
- 140+ day care procedures/surgeries
- AYUSH treatment
- Organ donor medical expenses
- Maternity
- Hospital cash ambulance and air ambulance
- Medical emergency reunion
- Vaccination for children
- Medical second opinion

Optional covers:
- Critical Illness
- Outpatient Treatment

## Eligibility Rules
1. Policy can be issued on individual sum insured basis.
2. Entry age of proposer: 18 to 65 years.
3. Maximum entry age of any individual: 65 years.
4. Children aged 3 months to 18 years may be covered only if parent(s) is/are covered at the same time.
5. Family members eligible include spouse, dependent children, and dependent parents.
6. Midterm inclusion at pro-rata premium is allowed only for:
   - Newborn between age 3 months and 6 months
   - Spouse within 60 days of marriage
   Other additions are allowed only at renewal.
7. Dependent children can port to similar retail health products at exit age.

## Sum Insured (SI)
- Plan A: ₹2,00,000 to ₹10,00,000 (9 slabs, in multiples of ₹1,00,000)
- Plan B: ₹15,00,000 / ₹20,00,000 / ₹25,00,000
- Plan C: ₹30,00,000 / ₹40,00,000 / ₹50,00,000

Optional cover SI limits:
- Critical Illness: ₹2,00,000 / ₹3,00,000 / ₹5,00,000 / ₹10,00,000 / ₹15,00,000 / ₹20,00,000 / ₹25,00,000
- Outpatient Treatment: ₹2,000 / ₹3,000 / ₹4,000 / ₹5,000 / ₹10,000

## Enhancement / Change of SI Rules
1. SI enhancement is allowed only at renewal.
2. Enhancement is generally to the next slab and at company discretion.
3. Waiting periods apply on enhanced portion as per policy exclusions.
4. Change of plan is allowed only at renewal, with 4 years continuous coverage and no chronic disease.
5. For plan change, medical reports are required for each insured person aged 40 years and above.
6. For individuals aged 70 and above, SI enhancement only in Plan A; plan change is not allowed.
7. Reduction of SI is allowed only in extreme hardship cases.

## Policy Period
1. Policy and optional covers are issued for one year.
2. Policy period for base and optional cover must be identical.
3. Policy should not be issued for less than one year.

## Proposal Form & Documentation
1. Proposal form is mandatory and must include age, height, weight, address/contact, email, PED, etc.
2. First part must be signed by proposer.
3. Insured person details and medical history must be completed separately for each insured person.
4. Annexure A required for adverse medical history.
5. Documentary evidence for address/age should be collected to prevent fraud.
6. Bank details and cancelled cheque/IFSC proof required for NEFT/RTGS claim settlement.
7. Proposal form is mandatory for:
   - New policies
   - Renewals with declaration changes
   - Portability in cases (with portability form/Annexure B)
8. PAN is mandatory for premium above ₹50,000.
9. If PAN unavailable, Form 60 or Form 61 must be submitted.
10. Photographs are required for ID issuance and fraud control.

## Pre-Policy Checkup (PPCU)
Pre-policy checkup is required for:
- Individuals aged 40 years and above, or
- Individuals opting SI ₹6,00,000 and above (irrespective of age), or
- Individuals opting Critical Illness optional cover (age 18-65 years)

If proposal accepted and premium realized, company reimburses 50% PPCU expense.

Required reports may include:
- Physical examination (doctor with minimum MD (Medicine))
- HbA1c
- Lipid profile
- Serum creatinine
- Urine routine/microscopic
- ECG
- Eye checkup including retinoscopy
- Any additional test deemed necessary

Report date should not exceed 30 days before proposal date.

## Declared Risk Factors
Proposal form includes and requires correct declaration of:
- BMI
- Smoking habit
- Alcohol consumption habit

## Permanent Exclusion / High-Risk Disease Mapping
Any existing illness under permanently excluded list should never be covered for specified ICD codes and must be mentioned in policy schedule.

Indicative list includes:
- Sarcoidosis (D86.0-D86.9)
- Malignant neoplasms (broad C/D ranges)
- Epilepsy (G40)
- Congenital/valvular heart disease, major heart ailments
- Cerebrovascular disease / stroke (I60-I69, I67)
- Inflammatory bowel diseases including Crohn's/ulcerative colitis (K50.x/K51.x)
- Chronic liver diseases / cirrhosis / alcoholic liver disease
- Pancreatic diseases including acute/chronic pancreatitis
- Chronic kidney disease / renal failure categories
- Hepatitis B
- Alzheimer's disease / Parkinson's disease
- Demyelinating disease (G35-G37)
- HIV & AIDS (B20-B24)
- Significant hearing loss disorders
- Papulosquamous disorders including psoriasis
- Avascular necrosis / osteonecrosis

## Premium & Discounts
Premium factors include age, SI, and optional covers.
Discounts:
- Youth discount: 10%
- Family discount: 10%
- Online discount: 5%
Discounts do not apply to optional cover premium.

## Renewal and Continuity
- Lifetime renewability.
- Renewal should not be rejected arbitrarily except fraud/moral hazard/misrepresentation/non-cooperation.
- Grace period: 30 days (coverage not available during grace period, continuity retained on timely payment as per terms).

## Cancellation
- Insurer may cancel for misrepresentation/non-disclosure/fraud with 15 days written notice.
- Insured may cancel with 15 days notice; short period premium table applies.
- No refund if claim admitted/lodged or benefit availed.

## Portability / Migration
Portability and migration allowed to/from policy subject to eligibility, acceptance, and extant regulations/circulars.
Continuity benefit for time-bound exclusions applies as per rules.

## Underwriting Notes
- Operating office may issue policy up to INR 10,00,000; above sent to RO.
- Panel doctors may be formed for PPCU evaluation.
- Adverse risks include chronic diseases like HTN, diabetes, kidney disease, hepatitis/liver disease, AIDS, cancer, genetic disorders.
- Critical Illness SI should not exceed inpatient SI.
- Proposal may be rejected if PPCU detects disease/illness likely to entail frequent claims.

## Interpretation Guidance (Medical Reports)

- Blood pressure classification
- HbA1c interpretation and caveats
- ECG interpretation scope
- Eye checkup/retinoscopy
- Serum creatinine ranges and causes
- BMI definition/classification and health consequences
- Lipid profile interpretation thresholds
