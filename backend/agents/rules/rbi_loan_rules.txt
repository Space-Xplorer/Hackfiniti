RBI / NHB / IRDAI — Loan & Housing Finance Compliance Rules (India)
=======================================================================
Source: RBI Master Directions on Home Loans (2023), NHB Guidelines, IRDAI Regulations
Effective for: Home Loans, Personal Loans, Business Loans

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1: BORROWER ELIGIBILITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 1.1 — Age of Borrower
The minimum age at the time of loan application is 18 years.
The maximum age at loan maturity shall not exceed 70 years for salaried borrowers and 75 years for self-employed borrowers.

Rule 1.2 — Income Documentation
All income claimed by the borrower must be supported by:
  (a) Last 3 months' salary slips for salaried individuals.
  (b) Last 2 years' ITR + CA-certified balance sheet for self-employed.
  (c) Form 16 issued by the employer for the last 2 years.

Rule 1.3 — CIBIL / Credit Score
Applicants must have a minimum CIBIL TransUnion score of 650 for standard home loan approval.
Applicants with a score between 600-649 require enhanced scrutiny and supervisor approval.
Applicants below 600 are not eligible for standard loan products.

Rule 1.4 — KYC Compliance
Mandatory KYC documents under RBI KYC Master Direction 2016 (as amended):
  (a) Proof of Identity: Aadhaar (UIDAI verified), PAN Card
  (b) Proof of Address: Aadhaar, utility bill (< 3 months old), or registered lease agreement
  (c) Aadhaar must be linked to the applicant's mobile number for e-KYC

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2: LOAN-TO-VALUE (LTV) LIMITS — RBI CIRCULAR DOR.STR.REC.51
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 2.1 — Home Loan LTV Ratios
  (a) Loan ≤ ₹30 Lakh:  Maximum LTV = 90% of property value
  (b) Loan ₹30L–₹75L:   Maximum LTV = 80% of property value
  (c) Loan > ₹75 Lakh:  Maximum LTV = 75% of property value

Rule 2.2 — Personal / Unsecured Loan
  No property collateral required. Maximum loan amount capped at 10× net monthly income.
  Maximum tenure: 60 months (5 years).

Rule 2.3 — Business / MSME Loan
  Maximum LTV for business premises: 75%
  Loan amount subject to MSME Credit Guarantee Scheme limits where applicable.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3: FIXED OBLIGATION TO INCOME RATIO (FOIR)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 3.1 — Maximum FOIR
RBI expects lenders to assess repayment capacity using FOIR.
  (a) For salaried borrowers: FOIR should not exceed 50%.
  (b) For self-employed borrowers: FOIR should not exceed 60%.
  FOIR = (Total monthly obligations including proposed EMI) / Net monthly income × 100

Rule 3.2 — Existing Obligations
All existing EMIs (home, car, personal loan, credit card minimum payments) must be included in FOIR computation.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4: INTEREST RATE & RESET CLAUSES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 4.1 — Benchmark-Linked Rates
Home loan and business loan floating rates must be linked to an external benchmark (RBI Repo Rate or MCLR).
ARR (Annual Reset Rate) must be clearly disclosed to the borrower.

Rule 4.2 — Prepayment Charges
(a) Banks may NOT charge prepayment penalties on floating-rate home loans to individuals.
(b) NBFCs may charge prepayment penalty not exceeding 2% of outstanding principal on fixed-rate loans.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING & SANCTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 5.1 — AML / CFT Obligations
All loan applications must be screened against:
  (a) PMLA (Prevention of Money Laundering Act) schedules
  (b) UNODC sanctions list
  (c) MHA (Ministry of Home Affairs) designated entities list

Rule 5.2 — PEP Screening
Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their immediate family members require enhanced due diligence (EDD) and senior management approval before loan disbursement.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6: FAIR LENDING & NON-DISCRIMINATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 6.1 — Fair Practices Code (FPC)
Lenders must follow the Fair Practices Code as per RBI Circular DBOD.Leg.No.BC.104/09.07.007/2003.
Loans must not be declined on the basis of:
  - Religion, caste, gender (outside government-mandated schemes), or region.

Rule 6.2 — Grievance Redressal
A grievance redressal mechanism with a designated internal ombudsman must be in place.
The Banking Ombudsman Scheme 2006 (as amended 2021) provides escalation to RBI Banking Ombudsman.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 7: DISCLOSURE & TRANSPARENCY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Rule 7.1 — Key Facts Statement (KFS)
Effective October 1, 2024 (RBI circular RBI/2023-24/68):
Lenders must provide a standardized Key Facts Statement to all retail and MSME borrowers:
  (a) Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
  (b) Total cost of borrowing
  (c) Recovery and grievance contact

Rule 7.2 — Credit Information Reporting
All disbursed loans must be reported to at least one RBI-licensed Credit Information Company (CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, CRIF).
Reporting must occur within 30 days of the event (disbursement, repayment, default).
